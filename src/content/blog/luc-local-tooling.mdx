The best local tools rarely feel ambitious at first. They remove a repeated command, preserve a bit of context, make an awkward workflow inspectable, or turn a manual checklist into a command you can trust.

Luc is a home for that kind of tooling.

## Small Tools Compound

Developer experience is often discussed through large platforms: portals, pipelines, dashboards, and integrated systems. Those matter. But a surprising amount of leverage sits closer to the terminal, where the work is still fluid and the feedback loop is short.

A good local tool does not need to own the workflow. It only needs to make the next action clearer.

## Local Means Inspectable

Local tools have a useful constraint: they have to be understandable by the person using them. They run near the files, commands, and state they affect. That makes them good places to experiment with automation before promoting an idea into shared infrastructure.

Luc is less a single product than a workshop for those experiments.

## The Taste

The taste is simple: prefer composable tools, explicit state, boring commands, and output that helps a developer decide what to do next.
