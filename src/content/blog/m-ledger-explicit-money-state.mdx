Financial software should be boring in the best possible way. The important questions should have direct answers: what happened, when did it happen, who initiated it, and what state changed as a result?

m-ledger is an exploration of that posture. Instead of hiding money movement behind clever abstractions, the system should make state transitions explicit and easy to inspect.

The engineering taste is close to accounting taste: preserve history, avoid ambiguity, and make reconciliation a first-class concern.
