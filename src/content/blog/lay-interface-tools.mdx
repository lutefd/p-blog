Technical interfaces often fail by being either too decorative or too raw. The good middle ground is quiet: dense enough to work in, clear enough to scan, and restrained enough that the content remains the main thing.

Lay is an experiment in that direction. It is a place to explore how layout, controls, and small visual decisions can make technical workflows easier to understand without turning them into a marketing page.
