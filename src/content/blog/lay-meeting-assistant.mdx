Meetings create a strange UI problem: the notes you need are often not the notes you want to show.

During a presentation or customer call, you may want a prompt, a rough answer, a private note, or a quick model response beside the thing you are sharing. But most desktop tools assume that if it is on your screen, it is fair game for the meeting.

Lay starts from the opposite assumption. It is an always-on-top meeting assistant built with Wails and Svelte, and the important product detail is that it stays off screen capture and sharing.

## A Small Window With Strong Opinions

The window is intentionally small and opinionated: frameless, translucent, always on top, opened at the top-right, with `520x360` as both the initial and minimum size.

On macOS, global hotkeys toggle the window, change opacity, and move it while focused. Those details matter because meeting tools live in interruption time. They should appear quickly, stay out of the way, and become more or less visible without dragging attention away from the call.

## Notes, AI Chat, And Gateways

Lay keeps the local pieces simple: notes in `~/.lay/notes.md`, config in `~/.lay/config.json`, a notes editor, AI chat, and model selection.

The gateway support is the more enterprise-shaped part. A team can embed a default gateway at build time or let users override it with `~/.lay/gateway.json`, then route all AI requests through a corporate proxy or model router.

## The Boundary Is The Product

The useful part is not that Lay has notes or chat. Plenty of tools have notes and chat.

The useful part is the boundary: private enough not to leak into screen sharing, small enough to sit beside a meeting, and configurable enough to fit a company gateway. That is a narrow product shape, which is usually where useful tools live.
